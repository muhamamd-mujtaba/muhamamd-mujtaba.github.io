Before my final result was announced, I applied to different universities with high hopes and strong expectations. Like many students, I had already imagined a clear path for myself and believed that things would work out according to my plans. However, reality was much harder than I expected. My entry test scores were not strong enough, and because of that, I could not secure admission in some of the universities I had wanted most. This situation created confusion, disappointment, and emotional pressure. I began to feel stuck between hope and uncertainty, because I did not know which option would finally be best for me. Some universities did not satisfy me, while others were completely out of reach because of rejection. During that phase, I started questioning my own ability and even felt lost about my future direction. It was difficult to stay positive when plans were collapsing one by one. Still, this period gave me an important life lesson. It taught me that not every journey begins smoothly, and sometimes we have to face rejection before reaching the right destination. Although it was a stressful and confusing time, it helped me grow mentally and accept that unexpected outcomes can also open the door to new opportunities.
